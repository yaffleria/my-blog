As someone living in Korea, let’s be honest: Coupang isn’t just an app; it’s infrastructure. It’s as essential as water or electricity. Recently, the news has been flooded with data breach reports. People are angry, politicians are talking, and the stock is wobbling. But as a long-time investor, I’m looking at this with a very cold, pragmatic eye. Here is why the "crisis" is a buy.

## Data Breach Fatigue

Let’s get real. Data breaches are not new. Just last year alone, major Korean entities like SKT and Shinhan Card had issues. At this point, I’ve accepted that my personal information is practically public domain. The news breaks, people get mad for a week, and then... silence. We go back to using our phones because we have to. To me, this news doesn't change my life. It’s just another Tuesday.

## "Infrastructure" is the Ultimate Moat

I could live without food delivery or streaming. But Coupang's core shopping experience? Non-negotiable. As a busy office worker, the last thing I want is to trek to a grocery store in freezing winters. Coupang gives me back my evening for $7 a month. Bom Kim wanted to create a world where we ask, "How did I ever live without Coupang?" That’s no longer a slogan; it’s literal truth. The moat is real because the alternative—waiting 3 days for shipping—feels like the Stone Age.

## The "Package Deal": Chips and Coupang

Here is the context you won't see in the headlines. PM Kim didn't just go to Washington for a photo op. He went there to save Korea's semiconductor industry from a potential **25% to 100% US tariff**.

The leverage is clear. Korea needs tariff exemptions for Samsung and SK Hynix, while the US demands a "fair playing field" for its firms like Coupang—and protection for their $350B investment promises.

It is highly likely that **Coupang is the chip bargaining chip**. The US can essentially say: "You want chip tariff waivers? Then stop harassing our e-commerce giant." This linkage makes the Coupang regulatory risk almost zero.

## The Lesson from "No Japan"

This reminds me of the "No Japan" boycott. Did sales dip? Yes. But the mood eventually shifted because you can't boycott irreplaceable technology or culture forever. The anger is real right now, but emotions eventually yield to necessity. You can boycott a brand for a month, but you cannot boycott an ecosystem that sustains your daily life.

## The Trade

I don’t care about the legal complexity. I care about the risk/reward. The fundamentals (retention) are unchanged, but the price dropped due to fear. Now, that fear is capped by US diplomatic protection. The downside is hedged by Uncle Sam, and the upside is fueled by the infrastructure monopoly. While everyone else focuses on the noise, I’m calculating if this is the right entry point.
